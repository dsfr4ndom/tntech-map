Henderson Hall

This Georgian Architectural Style building, designed by Benjamin F. Hunt of R.H. Hunt and Company, was commissioned by TPI President Quintin Miller Smith in 1929 to house the Engineering Department. Completed in 1931, it became the new model for all buildings to follow on the TPI campus, exemplifying its beauty with red brick and archways amidst the classic sidewalks and shade trees that characterize an ideal college campus.

The brilliant James M. Henderson formed the Engineering Department at TTU in 1924, before also becoming dean after forming the School of Engineering in 1949, where he served until his retirement in 1961. Henderson Hall was dedicated in his name in the Fall of 1961,an illustrious affair under President Derryberry’s watch.
